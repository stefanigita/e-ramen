Ikura Chawanmushi Ikura Chawanmushi is a traditional Japanese dish of steamed egg custard topped with salmon roe. It is served in a small cup or bowl and eaten with a spoon. The custard is smooth and silky, while the ikura adds a burst of salty and fishy flavor. Ikura Chawanmushi is a delicacy that can be enjoyed as an appetizer or a light meal.
Hamamatsu Gyoza It is a type of pan-fried dumpling filled with minced pork and cabbage, served with a soy-vinegar dipping sauce. The distinctive feature of Hamamatsu Gyoza is that the dumplings are arranged in a circular pattern and fried together, forming a crispy crust on the bottom. Hamamatsu Gyoza is a popular snack or appetizer that can be enjoyed with beer or sake.
Kaarage A hearty and satisfying dish, our black tofu ramen features chewy noodles in a rich broth made with soy sauce, garlic, and black sesame paste. Topped with crispy fried tofu, scallions, and nori, this vegan-friendly ramen will warm you up on a cold day.
Hiyayakko (Japanese Chilled Tofu) Hiyayakko is a traditional Japanese dish of chilled tofu topped with various condiments. It is a refreshing and light appetizer that can be enjoyed in summer or any time of the year. The tofu is usually served with soy sauce, grated ginger, green onions, bonito flakes and sesame seeds, but you can also customize it with your favorite toppings.
Okonomiyaki Okonomiyaki is a Japanese dish that consists of a wheat flour batter and cabbage, cooked on a flat griddle. You can add various toppings and ingredients to suit your taste, such as meat, seafood, cheese, or wasabi. served with a special sauce, mayonnaise, seaweed flakes, bonito flakes, and pickled ginger.
Agedashi Tofu Agedashi Tofu is a traditional Japanese appetizer made of deep-fried tofu cubes in a savory dashi broth. The tofu is coated with potato starch to create a crispy exterior, while the inside remains soft and silky. The broth is flavored with soy sauce, mirin, and bonito flakes, and garnished with grated daikon radish, green onions, and ginger.
